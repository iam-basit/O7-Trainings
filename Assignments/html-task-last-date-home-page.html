Dodge is an American brand of automobiles and a division of Stellantis, based in Auburn Hills, Michigan. It was founded as the Dodge Brothers Company by Horace and John Dodge in 1900. Initially, they supplied parts and assemblies for Detroit-based automakers before founding the Dodge Brothers Motor Vehicle Company in 1913, manufacturing complete vehicles. Here are some key points about Dodge: Product Range: Dodge is known for producing a variety of vehicles, including sports cars, muscle cars, SUVs, and minivans. Some of their popular models include the Dodge Charger, Dodge Challenger, Dodge Durango, and the Dodge Grand Caravan. Muscle Cars: Dodge is especially renowned for its powerful muscle cars that are equipped with high-performance engines. Models like the Dodge Charger and Dodge Challenger have gained a cult following among enthusiasts for their performance and retro-styling. Recent Developments: In recent years, Dodge has focused on enhancing its performance-oriented image, often emphasizing high-powered engines and aggressive styling. They have introduced variants with high-horsepower engines, such as the Hellcat and Demon editions, to cater to the performance car market. Technology: Dodge vehicles incorporate various technological features, including advanced infotainment systems, safety features, and driver-assistance technologies, to enhance the driving experience and ensure safety. Corporate Ownership: As of my last knowledge update in 2021, Dodge was part of the Fiat Chrysler Automobiles (FCA) group, which has since merged with Groupe PSA to form Stellantis, one of the world's largest automotive groups.
